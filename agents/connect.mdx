## Connect Claude, ChatGPT, or another AI app (MCP)

Perflo ships an MCP server, the open standard that lets AI assistants use external tools. Once connected, your assistant can see your Perflo tools and act within your permissions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your assistant's connector settings">
    In Claude: Settings, then Connectors, then Add custom connector. Other MCP clients have an equivalent screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Perflo MCP server">
    Enter the Perflo MCP URL shown in your app under Agent settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in and approve">
    You will be taken through a secure sign-in (OAuth) to link your Perflo account. You approve exactly which capabilities the assistant gets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a budget">
    Before the agent can spend, create a guardrail session: caps, allowlist, expiry. See [Agent spending and budgets](/agents/spending-and-budgets).
  </Step>
</Steps>

Then just talk: "what is my balance," "buy 2 grams of gold," "research this and email me a summary" all work in plain language.

## Connect a coding agent (CLI)

For developers and autonomous coding agents, the Grant CLI provides the same capabilities from the terminal. Generate a skill file from the app, paste it into your coding agent's workspace, and the agent can call Perflo capabilities and pay for third-party APIs per call, inside the same guardrails. No API keys to manage per vendor.

## Revoking access

Every connected app appears in settings with its own permission set. Revoke one connection, or hit the kill switch to stop all agent spending at once. Revocation is immediate.

<Warning>
Only connect assistants and apps you trust, and grant the minimum permissions the job needs. Guardrails cap the damage a misbehaving agent can do, but the best protection is a short allowlist and a low cap.
</Warning>
