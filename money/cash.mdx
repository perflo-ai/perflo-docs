Your cash balance is the center of Perflo. Deposits land here, trades settle here, cards draw from here, and payouts leave from here.

## What cash actually is

Cash in Perflo is held as digital dollars: USDT and USDC, the two largest dollar stablecoins, sitting in your own self-custodial account. The app shows one clean dollar balance; you do not need to think about which stablecoin or which network unless you want to. If you do want to, every token and chain is documented on [Networks and contracts](/reference/networks-and-contracts).

## What you can do with cash

- **Buy assets:** gold, BTC, or ETH, instantly at a quoted all-in rate. See [Gold](/money/gold).
- **Earn on it:** move idle cash into a yield vault. See [Earn](/money/earn).
- **Trade with it:** fund futures margin or prediction positions. See [Trading](/trading/futures).
- **Spend it:** fund a virtual Mastercard. See [Virtual cards](/cards-banking/virtual-cards).
- **Send it:** pay out to bank accounts in more than 45 currencies, or transfer to saved recipients. See [Payouts and transfers](/cards-banking/payouts-and-transfers).

## Receiving cash

You can receive cash three ways: bank transfers into your virtual accounts (including third-party payments from clients), card top-ups, and direct USDT or USDC deposits to your address. See [Fund your account](/getting-started/fund-your-account).

<Note>
Stablecoins are issued by third parties (Tether for USDT, Circle for USDC) and carry issuer risk. Perflo displays them as dollars for convenience, and you can always verify exactly what you hold on-chain.
</Note>
