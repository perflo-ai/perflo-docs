**Goal:** a Perflo account you control, ready for verification. Takes about a minute — all you need is an email address you can check right now.

Perflo accounts are self-custodial, but they work like a normal app. You sign up with your email address and a one-time code. Behind the scenes, Perflo creates a smart account that only you control.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Open the app or web app and enter your email address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the one-time code">
    We send a 6-digit code to your inbox. Enter it to sign in. This code is your login. Never share it with anyone, including anyone claiming to be Perflo support.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your account is created">
    A self-custodial smart account is provisioned for you automatically.

    **You'll see:** your account's home screen, and your account address under settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you should know

**There is no seed phrase.** Your keys are created and protected for you inside secure infrastructure that Perflo cannot read. You never need to write down 12 words, and there is no seed phrase for a scammer to phish.

**You own the account.** The address you see in the app is yours. Perflo operates services around it, but it cannot move your money on its own. Anything that moves funds happens under permissions you grant and can revoke. See [How Perflo works](/concepts/how-perflo-works).

**You never need gas.** Perflo sponsors all network fees. You will never be asked to buy ETH or any other token to use your account.

## Business accounts

A business can open an account the same way, then complete business verification (KYB) to unlock business banking, third-party pay-ins and payouts, and higher limits. See [Verify your identity](/getting-started/verify-identity).

## If something goes wrong

- **No code in your inbox?** Check spam, and confirm you typed the email correctly — codes arrive within a minute.
- **Signed in but everything is locked?** Money features unlock after verification — that's the next step, below.

<Warning>
Perflo staff will never ask for your one-time code, ask you to install remote-access software, or send you a deposit address in a direct message. If someone does, it is a scam. Verify deposit details only inside the app and verify token contracts on the [Networks and contracts](/reference/networks-and-contracts) page.
</Warning>
